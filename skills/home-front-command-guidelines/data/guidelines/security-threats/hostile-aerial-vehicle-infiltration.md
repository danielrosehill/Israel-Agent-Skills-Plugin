As part of the Home Front Command's new capabilities with providing an alert on a variety of threats, you can get an alert of a hostile aerial vehicle infiltration into Israel's airspace. Knowing how the alert is received and the behavioral guidelines can save lives.

There is a threat of a hostile aerial vehicle infiltrating the State of Israel's airspace. As part of the Home Front Command's capabilities to provide life-saving alerts and guidelines on a variety of threats, when the infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle into Israeli airspace is identified, an Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle alert will be received through the Home Front Command's means of alert. That way residents can act and protect their lives in accordance with the life-saving guidelines.

The infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle alert will be activated when the infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle into Israeli airspace is identified in any area that is in danger, and will allow you to act in time and immediately go to the most protected space — Mamad, Mamak or Mamam, shelter, inner stairwell or inner space. It is required to stay in the protected space for 10 minutes, unless another alert or additional guideline is received.

The infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle alert serves as a complementary pillar in the protection of civilians — knowing how you receive the alert and the behavioral guidelines during the infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle, can save lives.

## Means of Alert During the Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle

The alert for the infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle will be received using the Home Front Command's means of alert in accordance with the following:

### Home Front Command App

Provides a personal mobile alert depending on your location (location services must be activated) as well as in 10 areas of interest of your choice.

When you receive an alert for fear of the infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle, the caption "Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle" will appear on the screen along with guidelines on how to act to protect yourself. In addition, a short alarm will follow accompanied by an announcement: "Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle". The text and the announcement will be received in accordance with the language defined in the app or mobile phone — Hebrew, English, Russian or Arabic.

A special solution for the deaf and hard-of-hearing population: you can set to receive an alert by a 10 second vibration as well as by the flashlight indicator (on Android — set it in the settings page in the app, and on iPhone — vibration must be set in the device itself).

### The National Emergency Portal

Allows you to receive a targeted and personalized alert on your home computer, provided that the website is open on your computer and that you have defined the areas for which you want to receive an alert on the "Alerts" page. Upon receiving an alert for fear of the Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle, the text "Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle" will appear on the screen along with guidelines on how to act to protect yourself, in accordance with the language the portal is open on — Hebrew, English, Russian or Arabic.

### Home Front Command Sirens

The alert for fear of the Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle will be received using a rising and falling alarm (same as the alarm received in the case of a rocket and missile attack).

### The Media

The alert for fear of the Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle will be received on the national and regional radio stations by means of a designated sound, followed by the announcement "Infiltration of a Hostile Aerial Vehicle", the name of the area where the threat applies to and guidelines on how to act. Also, the alert will be received on the TV channels and some of the news websites. In addition, you can download the Home Front Command's App to Android based TV in order to receive alerts whilst watching any program.

## Hostile Aerial Vehicle Infiltration — Life-Saving Guidelines

### How should I act when receiving an alert about the infiltration of a hostile aerial vehicle?

Immediately enter the most protected space available — Mamad, Mamak or Mamam, shelter, inner stairwell or inner space. Stay in the protected space for 10 minutes, unless another alert or additional guideline is received.

### How should I act if an alert is received while I am inside a building?

Enter the most protected space available in the building — Mamad, Mamak or Mamam, shelter, inner stairwell or inner space. Stay in the protected space for 10 minutes, unless another alert or additional guideline is received.

### How should I act if an alert is received while I am outdoors?

**In a built-up area:** Enter the optimal protected space in a nearby building. Do not stay in the entrance hall.

**In an open area:** Lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands. If there is a limitation that does not allow lying down — crouch as much as possible and protect your head with your hands.

Wait until an explicit guideline is given allowing you to leave.

### How should I act if an alert is received while I am driving in a vehicle?

Stop at the side of the road, exit the vehicle, and enter the optimal protected space in a nearby building.

If a building cannot be reached: Exit and move away from the vehicle beyond the shoulder of the road or the safety railing, lie on the ground, and protect your head with your hands.

Only if you cannot exit the vehicle: Pull over to the side of the road, open the windows, and crouch below the window line.

### How should I act if an alert is received while traveling on public transport?

**On inter-city buses and school buses:** The driver must stop at the side of the road and open the doors; passengers must crouch below the window line and protect their heads with their hands.

**On an urban bus:** The driver must stop at the side of the road and open the doors so that passengers can get off and enter the optimal protected space. If the optimal protected space cannot be reached, crouch below the window line and protect your head with your hands.

**On a train:** The driver must slow down to 30 km/h; passengers must crouch below the window line in the carriages and protect their heads with their hands.
