# Terrorist Infiltration — Life-Saving Guidelines

> **Source:** https://www.oref.org.il/eng/life-saving-guidelines/terrorist-infiltration
> **Archived:** Thu Mar 20 2026 15:54:08 GMT+0200 (Israel Standard Time)
> **Original file:** `terrorist-infiltration.html`

---

During a terrorist infiltration event, immediately enter a protected space according to these guidelines:

## Indoors

- Lock the house door.
- Leave the lights on outside the house.
- Immediately enter the protected room (Mamad), close the door properly, and sit below the window line.
- If there is no protected room (Mamad) — enter a hideaway.
- Do not exit the protected space until you receive a message from the official authorities that the event has ended.

## Outdoors

- Immediately enter a protected place and stay there until you receive a message from the official authorities that the event has ended.

## In a Vehicle

- If you can continue driving, get to a safe location nearby as soon as possible.
- If it is not possible, stop your vehicle on the side of the road and seek shelter nearby that will provide you with maximum protection.
- Stay in shelter until you receive a message from officials that the event has ended.

## Important Highlights

- Traffic in the area is prohibited and entry is prohibited until further notice.
- If you receive an alert about a rocket or missile attack during a terrorist infiltration event — do not go to a protected space located outside the house, including the stairwell.
- Do not dismantle the protected room door handle. Removing the handle damages the mechanism and function of the door and its effectiveness in protecting human life. In order to block entry from the outside, it is possible to use effective and simple means that prevent the door from opening without damaging its integrity — such as barricading the handle with a heavy object.
- Do not share your location on social networks and through the various media channels.
- Stay updated by the Home Front Command's messages through official platforms and media channels.
